## 01 — Logline & Theme

### An elderly couple's daily ritual becomes an epic love language.

The world is a word puzzle — UNFOUND, ENFEOFF, FOUND, UNFED — but the only word that matters is the one piece missing.

Love is not solved, it is assembled. Childhood chase (pure, running) versus elderly patience (staying). The bench is the anchor across films. The garden holds time.

**Theme / Thesis:** "What we enfeoff — to invest with land, with home — is what we find."

ENFEOFF (v.) Old French: to put in possession of a fief, to endow.
UNFOUND → UNFED → FOUND.
From loss to nourishment to discovery.

*Handwritten margin note: "same letters, different love."*

## 04 — Puzzle Design Deconstruction

### The Core Mechanic: Hexagon of Love

Board layout: center F, surround C O E D N U.

Word tiles: FOUND ("What love discovers", active) · UNFED ("What neglect does") · UNFOUND ("The search") · ENFEOFF ("To invest with home", active) · FED ("Nourished") · ONE ("Becoming one", active)

Note: "ENFEOFF is the key — archaic, legal, beautiful: to give someone a home. That's marriage."

**Jigsaw variant — Map of Their World:** Jigsaw shows their village, river, baobab, home. One piece missing — the center. Puzzle as memory palace. When placed, map becomes whole again. (Note: "tactile cardboard, soft corners, slightly faded")

**Close-up crop notes:**
- Wood grain on honeycomb board — carved, not printed
- Letter tiles: warm ivory, ink stamp serif, soft shadow
- U piece slightly worn — thumb oil, held longest
