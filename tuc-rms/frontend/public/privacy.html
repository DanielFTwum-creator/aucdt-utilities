2. What Information We Collect

We collect information necessary to provide the Service and improve your experience:

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Account Information: Email address, password (hashed, never stored in plain text), name, role (registrar, lecturer, administrator)
  • Academic Data: Student ID, course codes, grades, test scores, class attendance, exam results
  • Contact Information: Phone number (optional), institutional email address
  • Communication Data: Messages, feedback, or support requests you send us

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, browser type, app version
  • Usage Information: Pages visited, features used, time spent in the application, search queries
  • IP Address & Logs: IP address, login timestamps, actions performed (logged for security audit purposes)
  • Cookies & Local Storage: Session tokens, theme preferences, language settings (localStorage only; we do not use tracking cookies)

2.3 Information We Do NOT Collect

  • Biometric data (fingerprints, facial recognition)
  • Location tracking (GPS, geolocation)
  • Social media information
  • Financial information (credit cards, bank accounts)

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your information for the following purposes:

3.1 Primary Purposes

  • Providing the Service: Creating user accounts, storing and retrieving grades, processing result submissions and approvals
  • Authentication & Security: Verifying your identity, preventing unauthorised access, detecting fraud
  • Communication: Sending service notifications, password resets, approval alerts
  • Audit & Compliance: Logging administrative actions for institutional and legal compliance

3.2 Secondary Purposes

  • Service Improvement: Analysing usage patterns to enhance usability and performance
  • Technical Support: Troubleshooting errors and providing customer support
  • Legal Obligations: Responding to lawful government requests or legal processes

3.3 Legitimate Interests

We rely on our legitimate interest in operating an efficient, secure results management system and protecting against misuse.

4. Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as necessary to provide the Service and comply with legal obligations:

  • Active User Accounts: Kept for the duration of your enrolment or employment at Techbridge University College
  • Grades & Academic Records: Retained indefinitely in compliance with educational record-keeping requirements
  • Audit Logs: Retained for 3 years for security, compliance, and dispute resolution
  • Session Tokens: Automatically deleted after 30 minutes of inactivity or upon logout
  • Deleted Accounts: After account deletion, personal identifiable information is removed within 30 days, but anonymised academic records may be retained for institutional records

When data is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymise it.

5. Your Rights Under Privacy Laws

5.1 GDPR Rights (EU Residents)

If you are located in the European Union, you have the following rights:

  • Right of Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal obligations)
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your data
  • Right to Object: Object to specific data processing activities
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: Contact your national data protection authority

5.2 CCPA Rights (California Residents)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Know: Request what personal information we collect and how it is used
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of personal information (with exceptions)
  • Right to Opt-Out: Opt-out of the sale or sharing of personal information (we do not sell data)
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We do not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights

5.3 GDPA Rights (Ghana Residents)

Under the Data Protection Act, 2012 (GDPA Act 843) of Ghana, you have the right to:

  • Access Your Data: Request and receive confirmation of what data we hold about you
  • Correct Your Data: Request correction of inaccurate personal data
  • Object to Processing: Object to certain processing activities
  • Data Portability: Request your data in a structured, common format
  • Lodge Complaints: File complaints with the Ghana Data Protection Commission

5.4 Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please email us at daniel.twum@techbridge.edu.gh with:

  • Your name and email address
  • A description of the right you wish to exercise
  • Proof of your identity

We will respond to your request within 30 days. If we cannot fulfil your request, we will explain why.

6. Data Sharing & Disclosure

6.1 Who We Share Data With

Your data is kept confidential and only shared in the following circumstances:

  • With Other Staff Members: Only staff with a legitimate institutional need (registrars, your assigned lecturer, administrators) can access your data
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose data if required by law, court order, or government request
  • Service Providers: We may share anonymised usage data with hosting providers (Plesk, Apache) for technical support

6.2 Data We Do NOT Share

We do not:

  • Sell personal data to third parties
  • Share data with marketing companies or analytics providers
  • Transfer data outside of Ghana without explicit legal authority
  • Use cookies for tracking or advertising purposes

7. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information:

7.1 Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption in Transit: All data transmitted to the Service is encrypted using HTTPS/TLS 1.3
  • Encryption at Rest: Passwords are hashed using bcryptjs (10 rounds), not stored in plain text
  • Database Security: Database credentials are restricted and access-controlled
  • Firewalls & Intrusion Detection: The server is protected by Apache firewall rules

7.2 Operational Safeguards

  • Access Controls: Only authorised staff can access data (role-based access control)
  • Audit Logging: All administrative actions are logged and monitored
  • Regular Backups: Data is backed up daily with geographic redundancy
  • Staff Training: All staff receive data protection and security training

7.3 No Breach Guarantee

While we implement strong security measures, no system is 100% secure. If a data breach occurs, we will notify affected individuals and relevant authorities within 72 hours as required by law.

8. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

8.1 How We Use Cookies

  • Session Cookies: Used to maintain your login session (expires after 30 minutes of inactivity)
  • Preference Cookies: Used to remember your theme selection (light, dark, high-contrast)

8.2 localStorage (Not Cookies)

We use browser localStorage to store:

  • Session JWT token
  • Theme preference (tuc_theme)
  • User language preference

8.3 No Third-Party Tracking

We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or other third-party tracking technologies. We do not track you across different websites.

8.4 Cookie Preferences

Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies or alert you when they are being sent. If you disable cookies, some features of the Service may not function properly.
